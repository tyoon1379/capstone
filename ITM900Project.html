2. Identification of the problem (s)/opportunities

2.1 Overview of the problem(s)/opportunities

ATSR is a chain of hardware stores that provide tools/equipment rental service for customers doing home improvement projects. However, due to its seasonal volatility, ATSR needs a new revenue stream that is more stable. As a solution, ATSR plans to expand the client base of the tools and equipment rental service to include major Canadian construction companies. This requires ATSR to transform its IS solution regarding the rental service to cater to the requirements of effective service provision to major business organizations as well as individual customers renting for personal project purposes.

Each location has a standalone desktop application called TRMS that handles all management related tasks and is composed of tool inventory database and customer account information. This system must be centralized in order to cater to the larger construction company since delivery will need to be made either from a centralized warehouse or in-stock inventory from each store. The in-stock availability of the tools could be automated via a website that is connected to the database server that has all the information necessary regarding the tool such as description, price, and in-stock availability of all locations combined. It is the task of ATSR to handle the logistics of pulling inventory from multiple locations and delivering the tools to the requested location. Once the client picks out the items to be rented out, a quote list can be submitted through the website which will then be confirmed by an ATSR agent who can personally reach out to the client via email about adjustable pricing, delivery specification, payment method and alternative items. In addition, the current walk-in customers should be serviced through the same website in order to alleviate the task of inputting customer information from the ATSR in-store clerk. The customer can select the tools needed before his or her visit and pre-arrange a pickup drop-off schedule via the website. The credit card information can be collected via the website as well in order to make the pick-up process more efficient and collect deposits in advance to mitigate the risk of the customer not showing up for pickup or drop-off. If the customer wishes to do a walk-in rental, the ATSR clerk at the store can go through the same TRSM process to register the customer in the same fashion as how it currently is. In essence, the new TRMS solution will provide a one-stop-shop for both construction company clients and walk-in customers.

8.Implementation and deployment & risk management plan for the new IS solution

8.1 Implementation and deployment activities

Implementation and deployment activities include the requirements for the project to be integrated within the existing framework. The implementation activities for this project would be:

  1. Assessing the project plan - in this phase we would analyze the requirements of the project and make sure the expectations are met with the proposed solution. This means all the stakeholders would be involved in deciding how the online store would function and whether the design encompasses all the requirements of the internal and external users, such as inventory management and online shopping capabilities.
  2. Execute the plan - This step would be the most involved, it would include creating the website and adding in the inventory of the store. It will require communication amongst all parties involved and to actually launch the website.
  3. Make changes - as with any project, there will be issues that arise. Perhaps a specific use case was overlooked in the design process, such as the function to filter tools by size, and this would require changes to the project process and refigured resource allocation.
  4. Testing - this includes making sure the website runs smoothly, that it updates the inventory, provides accurate quotes and has the ability to check customers out and process transactions. In this phase users will be able to run simulations on the website and make sure that the results are consistent before the official website launch.
  5. Gather feedback - this phase will occur after the website is launched, customers will be given the opportunity to provide feedback and point out any improvements that could be made to the system. Similarly, internal users will be able to assess whether the website is meeting their needs as well.
  6. Final reports - finally, the implementation is complete. A record will be created to document the process in detail and make sure that the project performed well against projected timelines, budgets and such. This helps assess the quality of the solution and make sure that the website brings in the business it was designed to.

In terms of deployment activities of the software, this will be specifically related to the website. The 4 main steps would be as follows:

  1. Plan and design - in this step, the analysts and developers will work to assess the requirements and design the website to meet them. This includes documentation and diagrams as we have outlined above and a project plan.
  2. Deployment - this is the stage where the website will be created and launched.
    For this step, the business will budget time and resources to roll out the website to the internal and external stakeholders. This will include training for the employees and the transition into a hybrid store space.
  3. Testing - this phase, shared with implementation activities, will include statistics on how the system is running such as clicks, views, orders, website traffic etc. This will be used to analyze the success of the website and recognize any changes that need to be made.
  4. Monitoring - in this phase the installation will be complete. This will just include monitoring the use of the website and making sure things are running smoothly - maintaining a status quo that works for the business.

8.2 Risk assessment and mitigation activities

For the implementation of the new system to go smoothly it is crucial to ensure that a solid risk assessment plan is put in place in order to mitigate potential risks and understand the impacts that these risks could pose to business operations.

Risk 1: Risk of data breach of customer information

Consequence level: Major

Likelihood: Possible

Impacts on business: A data breach is one of the most detrimental things that can occur to a company that is holding and securing sensitive data. If a data breach were to occur not only would that result in a loss of trust from the customer base but would also tarnish the reputation of the company and would result in people choosing to do their business with other competitors.

Mitigation strategy: To attempt to reduce the likelihood of a major data breach occurring it is first important to limit access to the data and only provide it to the people who it is essential to complete their work. Implementation of a strong firewall is also important to filter traffic within the system in order to identify malicious threats and block out attackers from accessing sensitive information within our systems.

 

Risk 2: Risk of loss of business due to system failure/downtime

Consequence level: Moderate

Likelihood: Unlikely

Impacts on business: In the event of a system failure or downtime it would lead to major disruptions in the business as customers would not be able to make new rental requests as well as also existing customers with current rentals would not be able to view or edit their orders.

Mitigation strategy: Although with any system the possibility of a system failure or downtime occurring is inevitable and needs to be accepted, the best way to reduce the likelihood and consequences of this occurring is through regular testing and monitoring of the servers within the system so that errors can easily be identified and resolved as soon as they are found. By performing regular backups of the server, it will ensure that damages can be reduced if servers are able to be back online as soon as possible.

Risk 3: Improper use of system due to user error

Consequence level: Minor

Likelihood: Possible

Impacts on business: If users or employees do not understand how the system functions, it would lead to more errors with orders which would increase the number of orders rejected. If employees are not trained on the new system, it could lead to delays with the delivery of products if the incorrect information is inputted into the system and if the system is not easy to use and understand for the customers it would turn them away if they do not know how to use it.

Mitigation strategy: To attempt to mitigate and avoid this risk it is crucial to ensure that employees are adequately trained on the new system so that they know its functions and know how to use it correctly. Along with this it is important that our system is extremely easy to use and user friendly so that new customers can easily make their selections and put their orders through the system.
